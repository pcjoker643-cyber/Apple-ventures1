Fuji Apples

Fuji apples are crisp, juicy, and sweet fruits originally developed in Japan. They are a cross between Red Delicious and Ralls Janet varieties. Known for their long shelf life and refreshing flavor, Fuji apples are enjoyed fresh, in salads, or baked, making them a popular choice worldwide.

Braeburn Apples

Braeburn apples are firm, crisp fruits with a balanced sweet-tart flavor. Originally discovered in New Zealand, they have red and green streaked skin and a refreshing aroma. Their juicy texture makes them excellent for fresh eating, baking, or juicing. Braeburn apples store well and remain flavorful for weeks.

McIntosh Apples

McIntosh apples are tender, juicy fruits with a mildly tart yet sweet flavor. Discovered in Canada, they feature red and green skin with a soft white flesh. Known for their aromatic fragrance, McIntosh apples are great for snacking, applesauce, and cider, though their softer texture makes them less ideal for baking.

Winesap Apples

Winesap apples are firm, crisp fruits known for their tangy, spicy flavor a hint of wine-like sweetness. They have deep red skin and dense, jwithuicy flesh. Popular in the United States, Winesap apples are excellent for cider-making, baking, and fresh eating, offering both rich taste and long storage life.

Russet apples

Russet apples are unique fruits with rough, brownish-golden skin and a sweet, nutty flavor. They are crisp, aromatic, and often slightly dry compared to modern varieties. Valued for their distinctive taste, russet apples are enjoyed fresh, in ciders, and traditional recipes, offering a rustic charm and old-fashioned apple experience.

Cortland

Cortland apples are crisp, juicy fruits with bright red skin and snowy white flesh that resists browning. Developed in New York, they offer a sweet-tart flavor perfect for snacking, salads, and baking. Their refreshing taste and attractive appearance make Cortland apples a favorite for both fresh eating and culinary use.
